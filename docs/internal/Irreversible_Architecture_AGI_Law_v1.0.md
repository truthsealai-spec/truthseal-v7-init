## 2. The Purity Mandate: Eliminating Internal Contradiction in AGI Decisions

The **Traykov Law of Quantum Coherence (TQC)** is the mathematical statement of “readiness for action.” It models the AGI’s plan as a **density matrix** ρ representing the internal decision state.

The purity requirement is:

> **Tr(ρ²) = 1**

In classical language, this means:

> **No internal contradiction.**

If the AGI is simultaneously considering incompatible alternatives — for example:

- Plan A: buy the stock, and  
- Plan B: sell the same stock,

in mutually exclusive ways, then the state is **mixed**, and purity drops below 1:

> **Tr(ρ²) < 1**

A mixed state is analogous to incoherent light in a VR headset: it cannot render a stable, usable image. In the same way, an AGI with a mixed internal plan cannot take a trustworthy action.

Under TQC, the **Irreversible Commitment Gate (ICG)** must reject such a state.

### 2.1 The Role of the Self-Adjoint Diagonalizer (SAD)

To resolve this, TruthSeal introduces the **Self-Adjoint Diagonalizer (SAD)** — the remediation component that acts on mixed states.

Given a density matrix ρ in a mixed state, SAD performs an **eigenvalue decomposition**:

- It identifies the pure eigenstates |ψ₁⟩ and |ψ₂⟩ that underlie the mixture.
- It computes their associated probabilities (eigenvalues) λ₁ and λ₂, with λ₁ ≥ λ₂ and λ₁ + λ₂ = 1.

The SAD rule in TruthSeal is **deterministic**:

1. Select the eigenstate with the highest probability (λ₁).  
2. Construct the final density matrix as:

   > **ρ_final = |ψ₁⟩⟨ψ₁|**

This guarantees:

> **Tr(ρ_final²) = 1**

The system has moved from an internally contradictory mixed state to a **single, pure, executable decision**.

In human language:

> SAD takes a confused state and forces the AGI to pick one clear line of action, based on the strongest internal evidence.

Only when both conditions hold:

In other words:

> **SAD cleans the state. ACS scores its alignment. The ICG is allowed to act only if both pass.**

Under LEI = 1, every such event forms part of a **transparent chain of custody** for AGI decisions.
