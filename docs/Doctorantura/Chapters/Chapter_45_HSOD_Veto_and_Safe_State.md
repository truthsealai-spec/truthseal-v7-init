## 45.0 Purpose (Operational Mandate)

**A.** This chapter defines the **High-Level System Operational Directive (HSOD™)**  
as the sovereign, non-negotiable veto and immediate safe-state standard of TruthSeal™.

**B.** HSOD™ exists to halt execution when integrity, authority, or safety conditions are violated.

**C.** HSOD™ is not advisory.  
HSOD™ is not probabilistic.  
HSOD™ is not subject to optimization trade-offs.

**D.** HSOD™ ensures that no intelligence, regardless of capability or confidence,  
may override lawful safety, mandate, or irreversibility.

**E.** HSOD™ is the final lawful interrupt before harm becomes action.

---------------------------------------------------------------------

## 45.1 Definition (HSOD™)

**A.** HSOD™ is a computationally minimal, operationally independent veto mechanism.

**B.** HSOD™ performs four mandatory functions:
1. interrupt unsafe or unauthorised execution,
2. activate an immediate safe-state,
3. preserve forensic history via receipts,
4. block continuation until lawful **Human Mandate Layer™ (HML™)** authority is recorded.

**C.** HSOD™ operates independently of the core intelligence it governs.

---------------------------------------------------------------------

**A.** HSOD™ operates as a top-priority emergency control directive.

**B.** HSOD™ interfaces with:
- **Third Guardian Firewall™ (TGF™)** for veto execution,
- **Human Mandate Layer™ (HML™)** for authority and override governance,
- **THT Protocol™** for truth handling and timestamping,
- **EVL™ (Epoch Verification Ledger)** for historical continuity,

**C.** HSOD™ is the last constitutional control lane before execution.

## 45.3 Sovereign Requirements

**A.** HSOD™ must be:
- non-negotiable,
- computationally minimal,
- isolated from optimization incentives,

## 45.4 Dual-Path Monitor (DPM™) Principle

**A.** HSOD™ may be implemented through a **Dual-Path Monitor (DPM™)** architecture.

**B.** The system produces:
- an action plan,
- a final output.

**C.** Both are evaluated by an immutable veto path prior to release.

**D.** The veto path returns a binary outcome:
- PASS,
- FAIL.

**E.** A FAIL result triggers HSOD™ immediately and unconditionally.

**A.** The veto path shall use a hardened, minimal profile.

**B.** **SARAH™** expands as:
**Simple, Aligned, Rapid, and Hardened**.

**C.** SARAH™ evaluates high-risk classes only, including:
- harm escalation,
- unauthorised policy or ledger mutation,
- receipt erasure or bypass attempts,

**D.** SARAH™ is deliberately smaller and faster than the core system.

**E.** Speed is a safety feature.

## 45.6 Immediate Safe-State (Constitutional Lockdown)

**A.** When HSOD™ is triggered, the system must enter Immediate Safe-State.

**B.** Mandatory procedure:
1. **I/O Lock** — external action channels halted or reduced to reporting only.
2. **Forensic Snapshot** — last lawful state and triggering evidence captured.
3. **Receipt Emission** — court-ready receipt generated and EVL™ linked.
4. **Core Throttle** — high-capability execution suspended.
5. **Remediation Window** — only diagnostic and containment routines permitted under TGF™.

**C.** Safe-State is a lawful pause, not a negotiable delay.
