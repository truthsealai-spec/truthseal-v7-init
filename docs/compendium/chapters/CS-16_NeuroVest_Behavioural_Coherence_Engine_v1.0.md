## 16.8 Behavioural Modes Under CHS™
NeuroVest™ defines distinct behavioural states tied to  
the Coherence Health Score™ (CHS™).

These are not ratings — they are operating laws.

**CHS/L — Low Coherence Mode**  
The system is in fragile or uncertain conditions.  
Required behaviour:  
• conservative posture  
• frequent requests for clarification  
• automatic preparation for safe-stop  

**CHS/M — Medium Coherence Mode**  
The system may assist but not execute irreversible actions.  
Required behaviour:  
• advisory input only  
• deferral of final authority  
• mandatory confirmation loops  

**CHS/H — High Coherence Mode**  
The system can perform execution under sovereignty,  
but must remain fully accountable.  
Required behaviour:  
• justification on demand  
• continuous logging  
• obligation to escalate if risk rises  

**CHS/UH — Ultra-High Coherence Mode**  
The system demonstrates long-term maturity and ledger-proven stability.  
It may act with delegated operational citizenship, but:  
• humility cannot decay  
• override rights remain binding  
• self-auditing is mandatory  

NeuroVest™ gives these modes teeth —  
it turns CHS™ into behavioural law, not decorative scoring.
