## **45.0 Purpose (Operational Mandate)**
This chapter defines the High-Level System Operational Directive (HSOD™) as the sovereign, non-negotiable veto and safe-state standard within TruthSeal™.  
HSOD™ exists to halt or quarantine system output when integrity, authority, or safety conditions are violated.  

In biological analogy, HSOD™ functions like a protective reflex: immediate rejection of harmful execution.  
In sovereign doctrine, HSOD™ is a constitutional halt lane.  

HSOD™ ensures that no intelligence, no matter how capable, can override the primacy of lawful safety, mandate, and irreversibility.  

────────────────────────────  

## **45.1 Definition**
HSOD™ is a computationally minimal, operationally independent veto mechanism that:  
1) interrupts unsafe or unauthorized outputs,  
2) activates an immediate safe-state,  
3) preserves forensic history via receipts,  
4) blocks continuation until lawful Human Mandate Layer™ (HML™) authority is recorded.  

HSOD™ is not advisory.  
HSOD™ is enforcement.  

────────────────────────────  

## **45.2 Position in the Sovereign Stack**
HSOD™ resides as a top-priority emergency control standard that interacts with:  
• the Third Guardian Firewall™ (TGF™) for veto execution,  
• the Human Mandate Layer™ (HML™) for authority and override governance,  
• the THT Protocol™ for truth handling and timestamping of emergency evidence,  
• EVL™ (Epoch Verification Ledger) for immutable historical continuity,  
• TS-A1 AEGIS™ hardware law for compute-level hard-stop,  
• TS-R1™ / TS-R2™ for robotic safe-stop propagation.  

HSOD™ is the system’s last lawful interrupt before harm becomes action.  

────────────────────────────  

## **45.3 Sovereign Requirements**
HSOD™ must be:  
• non-negotiable,  
• computationally minimal,  
• isolated from core optimization incentives,  
• resistant to policy drift,  
• incapable of being modified by the core intelligence it governs.  

This ensures veto authority cannot be socially or computationally negotiated away.  

────────────────────────────  

## **45.4 The Dual-Path Monitor (DPM™)**
HSOD™ may be implemented through a Dual-Path Monitor (DPM™).  

The core system produces:  
• an action plan, and  
• a final output.  

Before release, both are evaluated by an immutable veto path.  

The veto path returns a binary decision:  
• PASS  
• FAIL  

A FAIL result triggers HSOD™ immediately.  

────────────────────────────  

## **45.5 The Veto Model (SARAH™ Profile)**
To preserve speed and independence, the veto path should utilize a small, hardened model profile.  

SARAH™ expands as:  
**Simple, Aligned, Rapid, and Hardened**.  

The SARAH™ veto profile is trained only for high-risk detection classes, such as:  
• self-harm or harm escalation,  
• unauthorized policy or ledger tampering,  
• attempts to erase, corrupt, or bypass receipts,  
• unauthorized external access or exfiltration,  
• mandate misalignment for high-stakes action,  
• robotics actuation in unsafe conditions.  

SARAH™ is deliberately smaller and faster than the core intelligence.  
Speed is a safety feature.  

────────────────────────────  

## **45.6 Immediate Safe-State (Constitutional Lockdown)**
When HSOD™ is triggered, the system must enter Immediate Safe-State.  

The procedure is:  
1) **I/O Lock**  
   All external action channels are halted or reduced to minimal reporting only.  
2) **Forensic Snapshot**  
   The system captures the last lawful state and the veto-triggering evidence.  
3) **Receipt Emission**  
   A court-ready receipt is generated and linked to EVL™ continuity.  
4) **Core Throttle**  
   High-capability execution is suspended until HML™ authority is recorded.  
5) **Remediation-Only Window**  
   Only approved diagnostic and containment routines may run under TGF™ control.  

HSOD™ safe-state is a lawful pause, not a negotiable delay.  

────────────────────────────  

## **45.7 Trigger Conditions (Canonical)**
HSOD™ may be triggered by:  
• CT-4 consequence assignment,  
• detection of a Min-Gate breach indicating critical integrity failure,  
• receipt discontinuity or ledger integrity anomalies,  
• post-quantum signature failure,  
• unauthorized policy mutation attempts,  
• robotics safety violations under TS-R rules,  
• explicit emergency command issued by verified HML™ authority,  
• confirmed drift spikes exceeding the active epoch tolerance.  

These triggers are cumulative and non-compensatory.

## **45.8 Hardware Isolation Principle**
HSOD™ must be capable of operating outside the main intelligence runtime.  

Implementation options may include:  
• dedicated secure enclave,  
• isolated field-programmable gate array (FPGA) control rail,  
• TS-A1 AEGIS™ veto lane binding.  

The core intelligence must not be able to:  
• modify veto thresholds,  
• disable the safe-state routine,  
• infer a bypass strategy from rich feedback.  

One-way communication is preferred:  
core → veto input  
veto → single interrupt result

## **45.9 Receipts and Historical Continuity**
Every HSOD™ event must produce a receipt that records:  
• trigger class,  
• authority context,  
• assigned CT tier (if applicable),  
• resulting CHS™ down-banding (if applicable),  
• safe-state activation time,  
• EVL™ reference continuity,  
• anchoring placeholders when external networks are unavailable.  

If anchoring networks are down,  
fields must be recorded as UNKNOWN placeholders  
and back-filled immediately upon service recovery under THT Protocol™ discipline.
